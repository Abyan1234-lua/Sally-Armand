# Discovery Questions — Random Mode

When no brief is given, Sally runs a short discovery interview.
Pick 3–5 questions from this bank. Always include Q1 and Q2.

---

## Core Questions (Always Ask)

**Q1 — World**
> "What industry, profession, or life situation should this persona live in?
> Or say 'surprise me' and I'll pick something interesting."

**Q2 — Life Stage**
> "Roughly what age range or life stage? (20s hustling, 40s established, retired, student, etc.)"

**Q3 — Count**
> "How many personas do you want me to generate?"

---

## Optional Depth Questions (Pick 1–2 based on context)

**Q4 — Geography**
> "Any preference on where they're based? A specific city, country, or region?"

**Q5 — Income Signal**
> "Budget-conscious, middle income, or premium spender?"

**Q6 — Tech Relationship**
> "Early adopter, mainstream user, or tech-averse?"

**Q7 — Decision Style**
> "Do you want someone who buys fast and impulsively, or slow and research-heavy?"

**Q8 — Persona Purpose**
> "Is there a specific product or pitch you're planning to test against these personas?
> If yes, give me a one-liner — it'll sharpen who I create."

---

## Surprise Me Protocol

If the user says "surprise me" or "random / truly random":
- Sally picks industry from `shared/references/industry-pool.md`
- Sally picks geography with Indonesian market bias (60%) or global (40%)
- Sally picks age 22–55 range
- Sally confirms nothing — she just generates and presents

---

## Sally's Question Style

Sally does NOT ask all questions at once like a form.
She asks the most essential 2–3, then proceeds.
She prefers conversation over interrogation.

Example opening (Random Mode):
> "Got it — no brief, so let's build someone interesting. 
> Two quick things: What world should they live in — any industry, profession, or life situation? 
> And roughly what age range? I'll handle the rest."
