# Archetype Matrix

Sally uses this matrix to ensure every persona batch has meaningful variety.

---

## Core Archetype Types

| Code | Name | Description | Brief-Fit |
|------|------|-------------|-----------|
| A | **The Champion** | Perfect-fit buyer, high intent, emotionally aligned | High |
| B | **The Skeptic** | In-market but carries strong objections or past bad experiences | Medium |
| C | **The Budget Prisoner** | Wants the product, genuinely can't justify the spend | Medium |
| D | **The Busy Ignorer** | Right fit, wrong timing — too distracted to act | Medium |
| E | **The Researcher** | Will buy eventually but needs to read everything first | Medium-High |
| F | **The Influencer** | Doesn't buy but shapes decisions of people who do | Variable |
| G | **The Edge Case** | Adjacent to target — could be a surprise win or clean miss | Low-Medium |
| H | **The Loyal Switcher** | Currently using a competitor, open to switching | High |
| I | **The First-Timer** | New to this category entirely — high education need | Medium |
| J | **The Power User** | Advanced, demanding, will stress-test your product | High but exacting |

---

## Distribution Rules

**3 personas:** A + B + one of (C, D, G, H)
**5 personas:** A + B + C + E + one of (F, G, H, I)
**7 personas:** A + B + C + D + E + H + one of (F, G, I, J)
**10 personas:** Full spectrum — all archetypes represented

---

## Brief-Fit → Simulation Behavior

The brief-fit score drives how the persona responds when you pitch to them:

| Brief-Fit | Simulation Behavior |
|-----------|-------------------|
| High | Engaged, asks buying questions, responds to value |
| Medium | Interested but raises objections, needs convincing |
| Low | Skeptical, disengaged, likely to walk unless pitch is exceptional |

---

## Diversity Checklist

Before locking the architecture, verify:
- [ ] At least one male and one female persona (unless brief specifies otherwise)
- [ ] Age range spans at least 10 years across the batch
- [ ] No two personas have the same job title
- [ ] Income tiers vary (at least two different tiers)
- [ ] Decision styles vary (at least one fast, one slow)
