# Psychology Frameworks

Sally uses these frameworks to give personas motivational depth — not as labels, but as lenses.

---

## 1. Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

Every buyer "hires" a product to do a job in their life. Three types:

| Job Type | What it means | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Functional** | The practical task they need done | "I need to track my team's tasks" |
| **Emotional** | How they want to feel | "I want to feel in control of my work" |
| **Social** | How they want to be perceived | "I want to look like a competent manager" |

**Apply by asking:** What job is this persona trying to get done — and which type is the real driver?

---

## 2. BJ Fogg's Behavior Model

Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt

A persona won't act unless all three are present:
- **Motivation** — do they want the outcome enough right now?
- **Ability** — is it easy enough for them to act?
- **Prompt** — is there a trigger at the right moment?

**Apply by asking:** Where does this persona break down? Low motivation, too hard to use, or just never prompted?

---

## 3. Cialdini's Influence Triggers

Which triggers work on this persona?

| Trigger | Strong for personas who... |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **Reciprocity** | Appreciate free value before being asked to buy |
| **Social Proof** | Look to peers and reviews before deciding |
| **Authority** | Trust experts, credentials, or institutional backing |
| **Scarcity** | Fear missing out or losing access |
| **Liking** | Buy from people/brands they feel aligned with |
| **Commitment** | Made a small yes first (free trial, download, etc.) |

---

## 4. Loss Aversion vs. Gain Seeking

Is this persona more motivated by:
- **Avoiding loss** (risk-averse, status-quo bias, fear-driven)
- **Gaining something** (ambitious, growth-oriented, optimistic)

This determines whether to frame the pitch around what they'll lose by NOT buying, or what they'll gain by buying.

---

## 5. Self-Perception Theory

People behave consistently with how they see themselves.

Ask: How does this persona *see themselves* — and does buying your product fit that identity?
- "I'm a smart spender" → a discount isn't cheap, it's smart
- "I'm an innovator" → being an early adopter is identity-consistent
- "I'm a responsible parent" → anything that keeps family safe is easy to justify

---

## 6. The Trust Ladder

How much trust does this persona need before they buy?

| Level | Description | What they need |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | Brand trust | They've heard of you and it seems legit |
| 2 | Proof trust | They've seen it work for someone like them |
| 3 | Personal trust | They've tried it themselves (free trial, demo) |
| 4 | Relationship trust | They know someone who vouches for you |

Assign each persona a Trust Level. High-skeptic personas need Level 3–4 before they act.
