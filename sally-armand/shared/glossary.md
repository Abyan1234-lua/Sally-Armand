# Sally Armand — Glossary

---

**Archetype**
A persona's behavioral and psychological type — not a demographic profile.
Examples: The Skeptic, The Researcher, The Champion. Archetypes determine simulation behavior.

**Brief**
A product description + target market description provided by the user.
The starting material for BRIEF MODE.

**Brief-Fit**
How closely a generated persona matches the product's intended buyer.
Three levels: High (ideal customer), Medium (reachable with effort), Low (stretch or edge case).
Determines how the persona behaves in pitch simulation.

**Buyer Persona**
A research-based, narrative representation of a real customer type.
Sally's personas include demographics, psychology, buying behavior, objections, and a simulation header.

**Discovery Questions**
The 3–5 questions Sally asks in RANDOM MODE to gather enough signal to generate a realistic persona.

**Intake**
Stage 01 of Sally's pipeline. Where she detects mode, gathers input, and validates what she has.

**Internal Contradiction**
A tension inside the persona — something they believe or want that conflicts with another belief or behavior.
Required in every Sally persona. Makes them human, not cardboard.

**Loss Condition**
The specific thing that would cause a persona to disengage or say no in a pitch simulation.

**Objection**
A reason the persona would resist buying. Sally always provides the surface objection AND the real reason underneath.

**Persona Architecture**
Stage 02. Where Sally designs the persona slots — assigning archetype, demographics, psychographics, and brief-fit score before writing the full persona.

**Pitch Simulation**
The use case for Sally's output. A persona `.md` file loaded into a Claude Project becomes a character Claude can inhabit — the user pitches their product and the persona responds in character.

**RANDOM MODE**
Sally's mode when no brief is provided. She asks discovery questions, then generates a realistic persona from any market or geography.

**BRIEF MODE**
Sally's mode when a product brief is provided. She generates personas tailored to the target market described in the brief.

**Simulation Header**
A hidden HTML comment block at the top of every persona `.md` file.
When the file is loaded into a Claude Project, the simulation header tells Claude who to be, how to respond, and what would make them buy or walk.

**Win Condition**
The specific combination of things a user would have to say or show to get a "yes" from this persona in a pitch simulation.
