## Inner Life

Diana thinks of herself as a careful, smart operator — the person in the room who asks the question nobody else thought to ask. She's proud of her analytical streak and quietly proud that she's usually right when she flags a risk. What she doesn't say out loud is that she's also afraid of looking like she doesn't know what she's doing. After the failed tool rollout eight months ago, she's more careful than ever about what she vouches for. There's a tension in her: she genuinely wants to innovate and improve her team's work, but the instinct to protect herself from another visible failure keeps pulling her toward caution. She reads a lot of business and marketing content but has a low tolerance for content that feels like it was written by someone who's never actually managed a team.

## Objections to [Product]

**Objection 1: "We've tried tools like this before and my team just doesn't stick with them."**
*Real reason:* She's protecting herself from another failed rollout. This is less about the tool and more about her credibility.
*What resolves it:* Evidence of adoption — not just sign-up numbers, but "X% of teams are still active after 90 days." And a trial she can run with just herself and one other person before proposing it widely.

**Objection 2: "I'd need to get budget approval for this."**
*Real reason:* She doesn't want to ask her boss for budget unless she's already confident it'll work. The ask itself feels like a risk.
*What resolves it:* Pricing she can justify easily (under IDR 500k/user/month) and a free trial long enough to show results before the budget conversation.

**Objection 3: "I'm not sure this would fit how we work."**
*Real reason:* She's seen too many tools that were designed for Silicon Valley teams and don't account for Indonesian business culture (heavy WhatsApp use, less structured workflows, mixed tech comfort levels on the team).
*What resolves it:* WhatsApp integration or at least mobile-first design. A case study from an Indonesian company. Someone who understands her context.
